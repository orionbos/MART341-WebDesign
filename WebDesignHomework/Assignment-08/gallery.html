What is a Print?


The Process



Video: Woodcut Printmaking by "Artoholique" — Licensed Under CC Attribution License (reuse allowed).

The above video shows the woodcut printmaking method from start to finish.

It begins with a drawing on a panel of wood, otherwise known as the "matrix". First, you carve the martrix and then you roll the ink directly onto the matrix using a tool called a "brayer". Once thoroughly inked, you place the matrix on an etching press, register your paper atop it, place a hard surface--like a piece of compressed cardboard--atop the paper, and place a felt blanket atop the cardboard. You then turn a knob on the press and your sandwich is pushed through. On the other side, you remove the felt and cardboard from atop the paper and carefully remove the paper from the block. The carved image on the matrix that was coated in ink has now been transferred to the paper. The ink is still wet so you want to place it on a drying rack to dry. Repeating this process over and over creates an "edition" of prints.
